Threat-Level Protection (TLP) Compliance Requirement

As part of an ongoing platform-wide security initiative, Wix has introduced a mandatory Threat-Level Protection (TLP) compliance verification process, under which all hosted websites are assessed to ensure alignment with updated security standards aimed at mitigating evolving cyber threats and preventing unauthorized access to sensitive user data.

TLP represents a structured set of security configurations designed to protect critical information—including form submissions, login credentials, and payment details—from potential compromise; consequently, any failure to properly configure these protections may expose websites to significant risks such as data breaches, service disruption, and loss of user trust.

Websites identified as potentially non-compliant are required to take immediate action: users who have already implemented TLP must submit their configuration files for verification via compliance@wix.com, whereas those who have not yet configured TLP are required to complete the setup promptly, as non-compliance may result in temporary suspension until the required standards are met.

To facilitate compliance, users may connect with a designated specialist who will provide guided assistance throughout the configuration process, ensuring that all required safeguards are properly implemented.

All flagged websites must complete verification within the specified compliance period, as failure to do so may trigger automatic enforcement actions—including suspension—without further notice; this requirement applies universally and is essential to maintaining a secure, reliable platform environment.

Users are therefore strongly advised to act promptly in order to ensure continued website availability and uninterrupted service.
